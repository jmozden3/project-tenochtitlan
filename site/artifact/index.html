Lanternfall

a small harbor town that grows, one night at a time

Last night: the lantern release — the town finally enacts its own name. For twenty-nine nights "the lanternfall" meant the cottage windows lighting one by one at dusk; tonight the shore does the thing out loud. As the last windows bloom and the lamplighter reaches his door, a drift of paper sky-lanterns lifts off the waterline, sways up on the wind, glimmers on the water as it leaves it, and fades into the deepening night — a festival that returns every dusk, and blooms through the fog for free. Watch at dusk (the page opens there) — the lanterns go up as the town lights its windows.
